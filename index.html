Our story...

Vegan food has a rich and varied history that dates back centuries. While the concept of veganism itself may be a modern one, the idea of abstaining from animal products in favor of plant-based foods has been around for centuries. In ancient India, for example, followers of the Jain religion practiced a vegetarian diet, and some even abstained from honey and root vegetables. In the 1800s, a British couple named the Howells popularized the term "vegetarianism" and advocated for the use of plant-based foods. The term "vegan" was coined in 1944 by Donald Watson, who believed that a truly ethical diet should exclude all animal products. Since then, veganism has grown in popularity, with an increasing number of people turning to plant-based foods for health, ethical, and environmental reasons. Today, there is a wide variety of delicious and nutritious vegan foods available, ranging from fruits and vegetables to vegan meat alternatives and dairy-free desserts.
